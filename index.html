Our Process: Closing the Loop on 3D Waste

1. Collection & Sorting

Gathering wasted 3D prints (failed prints, supports, prototyping waste) from users and 3D printing services, then sorting them by plastic type (PLA, PETG, ABS, etc.).

2. Shredding & Melting

The plastic waste is sterilized, shredded into pellets, and then melted down. Impurities are removed to ensure a clean, high-quality material base.

3. Extrusion & Re-Use

The melted material is extruded into new, recycled 3D printer filament or repurposed into other useful products, completing the circular lifecycle.

Additive manufacturing is using recycled plastic as its raw material for many 3D Printing machines. This method is particularly suitable for brands wanting to make their supply chain more sustainable, especially in combination with 3D printing technology for production on an industrial scale. Compared to virgin material, the filament using recycled material has a lower carbon footprint. The product is also more durable and meets industry-specific requirements.
